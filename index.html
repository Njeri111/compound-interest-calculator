Compound Interest — Frequently Asked Questions

What is compound interest? +

Compound interest is interest calculated on both your principal and previously earned interest. Unlike simple interest, which only calculates on the original amount, compound interest causes your money to grow exponentially — the longer you invest, the faster it grows. This is why starting early is so powerful.

What is the compound interest formula? +

The standard formula is A = P(1 + r/n)nt, where A is the final amount, P is the principal, r is the annual interest rate (as a decimal), n is the number of compounding periods per year, and t is time in years. For regular contributions, this calculator simulates each period individually for accuracy.

How often should interest compound for the best returns? +

The more frequently interest compounds, the higher your returns — daily compounding yields slightly more than monthly or annually. However, the difference between daily and monthly compounding is often very small. Your interest rate, contribution amount, and time invested have a far greater impact on your final balance than compounding frequency alone.

What is the Rule of 72? +

The Rule of 72 is a quick shortcut: divide 72 by your annual interest rate to estimate how many years it takes to double your money. For example, at 8% interest, your investment doubles approximately every 9 years (72 ÷ 8 = 9). Use this calculator to see the exact growth curve for your specific scenario.

How much do I need to invest monthly to reach $1,000,000? +

It depends on your rate of return and time horizon. At an 8% annual return over 30 years, investing around $670/month reaches $1 million. Over 40 years, just $286/month does the same — showing how powerful starting early is. Use the calculator above to model your exact path to any target.
